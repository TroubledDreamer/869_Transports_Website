Activities

Some of the best places to visit in St. Kitts

Brimstone Hill Fortress National Park

Brimstone Hill Fortress National Park is a clear illustration of 17th- and 18th-century military architecture in the Caribbean. The fortress, designed by the British and constructed by African slave labor, bears witness to European colonial expansion, the African slave trade, and the formation of new communities in the Caribbean.

Open Daily 9:30am -5:30pm
Entrance Fees: US$15.00
Children Under 10 Free


Mt. Liamuiga Volcano Hike

Enjoy this 6.1-kilometer out-and-back trail near Saint Paul Capisterre. It is generally viewed as a difficult route, and it takes an average of 2 hours and 40 minutes to finish. Although this is a popular trail for birding, hiking, and strolling, you can still find privacy during quieter times of day. The route is available all year and is a great place to come at any time. Dogs are welcome and can roam freely in some locations.

Lentgh: 6.1 km
Elevation gain: 638m
Route Type: Out & back


The St. Kitts Scenic Railway

The St. Kitts Scenic Railway offers a 3-hour tour that round the beautiful Eastern Caribbean island of St. Kitts, covering 18 miles by narrow gauge railway and 12 miles by sightseeing buses. The "Last Railway in the West Indies," built between 1912 and 1926 to move sugar cane from the island's sugar farms to the sugar refinery in the capital city of Basseterre, now offers travelers an unrivaled opportunity to explore the nature and culture of this unspoiled country.

Upon arrival by cruise ship The Scenic Railway tour tickets can be purchased from your cruise line.

Clay Villa Plantation
House & Gardens

Clay Villa Plantation House and Gardens in Basseterre is a 10-acre (7.5-hectare) property. Built in 1763 and owned by a family of Carib Indians whose 7th-generation descendants are the current proprietors, the estate is famous for being a free working plantation while others surrounding it profited from the slave trade.

Location: Basseterre
Time: Varies


St. Kitts Eco-Park

The St. Kitts Eco-Park is a hidden treasure on the western end of the island that takes visitors beyond stretches of enticing white-sand beaches to tangerine-painted sunrises and sunsets in a setting of twenty (20) expansive acres of serene, landscaped gardens and lush mountain ridges in the background; home of the now-dormant Mount Liamuiga volcano.
